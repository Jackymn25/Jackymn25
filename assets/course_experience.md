# My Course Experience & Course Reviews

## 中文版

这份记录整理了我从 2024 Summer 到 2026 Fall 的课程经历与个人评价。内容主要基于我的真实体验，包括课程实用性、难度、工作量，以及我个人对授课方式、考核形式和课程价值的主观看法。仅供参考，不同同学的体验可能会有所不同。

---

## 2024 Summer

### ISP010 (online)
我个人感觉这门课其实不是很必要。我当时听说 ISP100 有入学考试，有点担心过不了，所以提前上了这门课，算是给自己一个保险，保证能上 ISP100。

不过回头看，好像确实没什么必要。这门课整体非常简单，内容集中，差不多三小时就能搞完，而且**没有学分**。

## 2024 Fall

**GPA: 3.93**

### MAT102H5 — A-
- **实用性：** 3/10  
- **难度：** 7/10  
- **课程工作量：** 6/10  

这门课主要学习数学证明。我个人感觉对计算机来说实用性不大，除了数学归纳法等很基础的证明思维之外，整体价值比较低，更多像是一门筛选课程。

Tyler Holden 的确比较严格。我有些朋友感觉已经很厉害了，最后也基本只是 90+。不过如果认真学，想达到 make post 其实还是很轻松的。想拿 4.0 会有一定难度。

### MAT135H5 — A+
- **实用性：** 6/10  
- **难度：** 2/10  
- **课程工作量：** 4/10  

这门课是基础微积分，整体感觉和高中学过的内容比较像。如果对数学有兴趣，我会更建议学 MAT137；如果兴趣一般，那这门课也完全够用。Ivan 人很好。

### ISP100H5 — A
- **实用性：** 2/10  
- **难度：** 5/10  
- **课程工作量：** 5/10  

主要是写作课。作业给分挺看老师，对像我这样英语是第二语言的同学会有一定难度，不过整体还可以。建议多去 office hour 和老师交流，分数通常会更好看一点。

课程本身有点枯燥，尤其是一口气上三个小时。作业期间压力会比较大，总会想着怎么把文章再改得更好一些。

### CSC108H5 — A
- **实用性：** 7/10  
- **难度：** 4/10  
- **课程工作量：** 6/10  

这门课学 Python，难度整体不高，我因为本身有一些基础，所以在程序控制部分压力不大。函数、类、字典这些内容需要熟悉，考试也会手写代码。

大作业的工作量有点大，但整体是一门不错的入门课。

### ENV100Y5 — CR
- **实用性：** 0/10  
- **难度：** 8/10  
- **课程工作量：** 3/10  

建议避开这个老师。考试非常难，课程是线上课，内容也极其枯燥。

## 2024 Summer

### ISP010 (online)
Personally, I do not think this course was very necessary. At the time, I heard that ISP100 required an entrance assessment, and I was worried that I might not pass, so I took ISP010 in advance just to make sure I could get into ISP100.

Looking back, it probably was not necessary after all. The course was very easy, highly condensed, and could basically be finished in about three hours. It also carried **no credit**.

## 2024 Fall

**GPA: 3.93**

### MAT102H5 — A-
- **Usefulness:** 3/10  
- **Difficulty:** 7/10  
- **Workload:** 6/10  

This course focused on mathematical proofs. In my opinion, it was not very useful for computer science beyond basic proof thinking such as mathematical induction. Overall, its practical value felt low and it seemed more like a filtering course.

Tyler Holden was indeed quite tough. Some of my friends were already very strong students and still only ended up around the low 90s. That said, if you study seriously, making post is still quite easy. Getting a 4.0, however, takes some effort.

### MAT135H5 — A+
- **Usefulness:** 6/10  
- **Difficulty:** 2/10  
- **Workload:** 4/10  

This was a basic calculus course, and it felt very similar to high school material. If you are genuinely interested in math, I would recommend MAT137 instead. If not, MAT135 is more than enough. Ivan was a nice instructor.

### ISP100H5 — A
- **Usefulness:** 2/10  
- **Difficulty:** 5/10  
- **Workload:** 5/10  

This was mainly a writing course. Grading depends a lot on the instructor, and for someone like me, whose English is a second language, that made it somewhat challenging. Still, it was manageable overall. I would recommend going to office hours often, because talking to the instructor usually helps your grades look better.

The course itself was a bit boring, especially with three-hour sessions at a time. The assignments could also feel stressful because I was constantly thinking about how to improve my writing further.

### CSC108H5 — A
- **Usefulness:** 7/10  
- **Difficulty:** 4/10  
- **Workload:** 6/10  

This course taught Python. Overall, the difficulty was not high. Since I already had some background, I did not feel much pressure with basic programming control. You should be comfortable with functions, classes, dictionaries, and similar topics. The exams also required handwritten code.

The major assignment had a fairly heavy workload, but overall it was a good introductory course.

### ENV100Y5 — CR
- **Usefulness:** 0/10  
- **Difficulty:** 8/10  
- **Workload:** 3/10  

I would recommend avoiding this instructor. The exams were very hard, the course was online, and the content was extremely boring.
